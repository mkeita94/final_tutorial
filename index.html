Why is it so important to predict stroke?¶

A stroke is a cardiovascular disease that affects brains' cells when its tissues lack oxygen. According to the CDC, over 795,000 people in the United States have stroke every year. In additon, someone dies of stroke every 40 seconds in the United States. Since this situation is critical, it is necessary to have an idea about the proportion of the population who is mostly affected by this disease and predict who might get it based on the current data. In this case, I define proportion as gender, high BMI, smokers, and people with underlining conditions.

https://www.cdc.gov/stroke/facts.htm

Data Collection¶

We will be using a dataset "Stroke Prediction dataset" that we downloaded as a csv(comma separated values) file from the website https://www.kaggle.com/fedesoriano/stroke-prediction-dataset. This dataset has 12 essential features(variables) with 5110 observations representing information collected from several patients.

Here is the detail of features representing each column and its expected value.

1) id: unique identifier

2) gender: "Male", "Female" or "Other"

3) age: age of the patient

4) hypertension: 0 if the patient doesn't have hypertension, 1 if the patient has hypertension

5) heart_disease: 0 if the patient doesn't have any heart diseases, 1 if the patient has a heart disease

6) ever_married: "No" or "Yes"

7) work_type: "children", "Govt_jov", "Never_worked", "Private" or "Self-employed"

8) Residence_type: "Rural" or "Urban"

9) avg_glucose_level: average glucose level in blood

10) bmi: body mass index

11) smoking_status: "formerly smoked", "never smoked", "smokes" or "Unknown"*

12) stroke: 1 if the patient had a stroke or 0 if not

*Note: "Unknown" in smoking_status means that the information is unavailable for this patient

This is an interesting dataset since it contains important information that will help us with our prediction.

Since we now have the dataset available, we will use the library called pandas which will enable us to store, clean and analyze it.

Conclusion¶

As I mentioned at the beginning of this tutorial, stroke is a serious disease that is among the top causes of death. However, it is challenging to have accurate model to predict stroke. It can be caused by several issues, such as high bmi(obesity), heart disease or age. In addition, our model predicts that there is a chance to have a stroke as people get older. On the other hand, I have learned about several aspects of stroke. The patients' data contains important features, such as bmi, age, hypertension, and heart_disease. Through exploratory data analysis I found several features that correlated with age.

Futhermore, I decided to use both linear regression and DecisionTreeClassifier to help with predictions by testing whether the relationship between the predictors and targets could be used to accurately predict the targets. However, the decisionTreeClassifier gave a better result compared to the linear regression.

Another important thing that I noticed is the correlation between age and bmi. Even if we are more focusing on stroke, high bmi could be a source of a lot of diseases. However, I think that further investigations should be done to have answers to major questions related to stroke.
